# Gate and tests quick reference

One screen to self-check a reading before presenting it. Run the gate, then the tests, then confirm none of the six failure modes are present. Every line here is derived from the comprehension method, this is the compressed form, not new rules.

---

## The comprehension gate (run on the dossier before presenting)

Nine checks. Any fail triggers rework, not progression.

1. **Function over form.** Each unit is translated to the work it demands, not the words it uses. A unit satisfied by naming the requirement fails.
2. **Unknown over invention.** Every gap is stated with its impact and what would resolve it. Nothing is fabricated to fill a field. An admitted unknown beats invented specificity.
3. **Depth over labels.** No high-risk unit is a one-liner. Each clears the exemplar depth.
4. **Completeness, no silent omissions.** Every meaningful unit has a row. Anything skipped is a conscious, stated choice, never an accident.
5. **Connected reading.** Each unit names how it relates to the whole. The document is read as one connected ask, not a pile of isolated clauses.
6. **On its own terms (de-bias).** The document is read for what it asks before it is mapped to any preferred answer or solution. A reading bent toward a desired output is sent back.
7. **Evidence honesty.** Load-bearing claims carry a confidence tag and a source; anything that will carry weight downstream is marked for verification before it is relied on.
8. **Scope fidelity (no drift).** The reading stays an answer to *this* document. Places where a plausible response would drift off-topic are flagged.
9. **Stop for confirmation.** The dossier is presented as a checkpoint, and work stops for the user to confirm or correct before anything is acted on.

---

## The function tests (apply per unit, and again whenever a reading is revised)

Literalism creeps back into filled templates, so re-apply these at each pass.

- **Function test:** does this perform the work the unit demands, or only name it? *Fails when* the criteria appear as labels with no assessment behind them.
- **Label test:** could this pass by using the right words without any insight? *Fails when* a stock phrase is appended to every item unchanged.
- **So-what test:** why does this matter to the goal or the reader? *Fails when* a fact is stated with no implication, or a finding has no stated consequence for a decision.
- **No-faking test:** are all unknowns recorded as unknowns, with nothing invented to clear a field? *Fails when* a detail, figure, mechanism, or date is fabricated to pass a depth check.
- **De-bias test:** is each unit read on its own terms before being mapped to a preferred answer? *Fails when* the reading is framed around the output you wanted to give.
- **Cohesion test:** can a reader follow the units as one connected reading of the document? *Fails when* a unit is a disconnected fragment with no link to the whole.
- **Scope test:** does this stay an answer to *this* document, in scope? *Fails when* the work is deep but answers a different question.

---

## The six failure modes this skill guards against

A reading is defective if it shows any of these:

- **Bias** — the reading bent toward a preferred answer instead of taking the document on its own terms first.
- **Shallowness** — one-liners and labels where the document demands analysis.
- **Fabrication** — invented specifics passed off as grounded; an admitted unknown is always better.
- **Unexplained labels** — a verdict, rank, or stance with no reasoning a reader can reconstruct.
- **Logical errors** — a conclusion the document does not support; an order derived by averaging rather than by the decisive factor.
- **Brief-drift** — deep work that answers a different question than the one asked; the most dangerous failure because it can look polished.

## Evidence-tag legend

- `[V]` **verified** — primary or named, cross-checkable source. Required for any claim that will carry weight downstream.
- `[I]` **informed** — one credible secondary source; acceptable for context and framing, may not be load-bearing.
- `[?]` **open** — unknown or unverified. State it, its impact, and what would close it.

External, regulatory, or time-sensitive claims carry an as-of date and a still-current check, so a stale or misremembered point cannot be relied on.
